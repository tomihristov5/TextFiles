Manchester United Football Club is an English professional football club, based in Old Trafford, Greater Manchester that plays in the Premier League. 
Founded as Newton Heath LYR Football Club in 1878, the club changed its name to Manchester United in 1902 and moved to Old Trafford in 1910.
Manchester United have won the most League titles (20) of any English club,[3] a joint record 11 FA Cups, four League Cups, and a record 20 FA Community Shields. 
The club has also won three European Cups, one UEFA Cup Winners' Cup, one UEFA Super Cup, one Intercontinental Cup, and one FIFA Club World Cup. 
In 1998–99, the club won a continental treble of the Premier League, the FA Cup and the UEFA Champions League.
